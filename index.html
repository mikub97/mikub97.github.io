Bio

Hello everyone! My name is MichaÅ‚ Weiss. I am a cognitive scientist, software engineer, and educator. My research focuses on embodied cognition and multimodal interaction. I study how language, gaze, and movement synchronize in collaborative activities, and how these processes can inform the design of digital systems such as collaborative robots and educational technologies. I collaborate closely with the Human Interactivity and Language Lab (HILL) and the Copernicus Science Centre in Warsaw, where I work on projects connecting interaction research with learning sciences.

I hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science from the Warsaw University of Technology and an M.Sc. in Cognitive Science from the University of Warsaw. Currently, I conduct research on embodied learning with the Copernicus Science Centre, studying how children develop Cartesian perception through joint action and interactive games.

Alongside my academic work, I have more than eight years of teaching experience. I have taught mathematics and computer science in Classical School (formerly Akademeia Tutoring College), Liceum w Chmurze, and L.O. im. Zuzanny Ginczanki. I enjoy inspiring students by recognizing their talents and helping them broaden their horizons. I currently offer online lessons in mathematics and computer science for learners at all levels, from primary school to university.

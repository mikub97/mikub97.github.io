The house on Rua das Laranjeiras where Manoel dos Reis Machado taught is now a small museum. We visited in the late afternoon when the heat finally softened.

<!--more-->

The training space is smaller than I imagined. Wooden floor, low ceiling, photographs covering most of one wall. Someone had left a single flower by the entrance.

What strikes you, standing in a place like this, is the gap between the scale of a person's life and the scale of their impact. The room is perhaps eight meters across. From this room, or rooms like it, a way of moving through the world was transmitted, refined, argued over, and carried to every continent.

Embodied knowledge doesn't leave traces the way written knowledge does. There are no manuscripts. There is only what people remember in their bodies.

![Interior of Casa de Mestre Bimba](/assets/images/bimba-house.jpg)

I kept thinking about what Sheets-Johnstone wrote about kinesthesia as primary — that the body knows first, in a register that language is always translating after the fact.

Standing here, that felt more true than it does in a library.
